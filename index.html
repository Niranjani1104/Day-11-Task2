Kazi Nazrul Islam

The National poet of Bangladesh

Rebel Poet

Kazi Nazrul Islam (24 May 1899 - 29 August 1976), also known as the Rebel Poet, was a popular Bengali poet, writer, lyrist and the national poet of Bangladesh.[1] Nazrul produced a large body of poetry, music, messages, novels, and stories with themes that included equality, justice, anti-imperialism, humanity, rebellion against oppression and religious devotion.[2] Nazrul Islam's activism for political and social justice as well as writing a poem titled as "Bidrohī", meaning "the rebel" in Bengali, earned him the title of "Bidrohī Kôbi" (Rebel Poet)
